Scoring Reference Guide
Use the same score for Volume and Complexity unless they differ meaningfully. When in doubt, score what you see — don't adjust for what the client tells you.
Volume — How much is in the room?
Score What it looks like
1 Sparse — Nearly empty. Vacation/seasonal property, recently decluttered, or minimal furnishings. Walkways fully clear, minimal closet contents.
2 Light — Below-average volume. Orderly, easy access throughout. Closets manageable. Short-term occupancy or active decluttering already underway.
3 Standard — Normal household accumulation. Fully furnished, closets reasonably full, some storage overflow. Typical long-term primary residence. Default score.
4 Dense — Above-average accumulation. Storage areas overflowing, significant closet/garage volume, multiple hobby or collection areas. Pathways clear but room movement is constrained.
5 Extreme — Floor-to-ceiling accumulation. Obstructed pathways (ICD Level 3+). Hoarder-level density. Requires escalation to Anthony Sr before estimating. Do not use default hours — price separately.
Complexity — How difficult is the handling?
Score What it looks like
1 Simple — Common household items only. No fragile, high-value, or specialty items. Straightforward sort and remove.
2 Moderate — Some fragile items (china, glassware), standard furniture requiring care, basic sorting decisions with a cooperative client or executor.
3 Standard — Mix of fragile, sentimental, and moderately valuable items. Some appraisal coordination expected. Normal family dynamics. Default score.
4 High — Significant art, antiques, jewelry, wine, or collections requiring appraiser coordination. Multiple beneficiaries with competing interests. Documented chain-of-custody required throughout.
5 Specialist — Firearms, cryptocurrency, active business interests, contested probate, biohazard, or multiple high-value categories requiring licensed specialist coordination. Escalate to Anthony Sr immediately.
Remember: Score 3 is the starting point for a normally furnished Palm Beach home. Score up if you see density or complexity above average. Score down for condos, vacation properties, or recently downsized spaces. When Vol and Complexity differ significantly, score each independently — the app handles them separately.
